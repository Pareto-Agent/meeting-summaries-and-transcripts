The team established a policy to cease new code pushes until after the demo with AJ to maintain stability.
* Pipeline Stage Definitions: Parth Goda Rajesh clarified the refined structure of the outreach funnel: monitoring, draft state, waiting for response, engaged, unreachable, and finally the proposal stage. This structure aims to clarify the trigger points for emails and the progression of deals based on client interaction or lack thereof.
* Overview Dashboard Metrics and Filtering: Aaron Rose, Evan Liang, and Parth Goda Rajesh discussed the overall dashboard metrics, specifically the response rate of 23% and the need for a global filter that applies across the entire website when a user selects a specific period like Q3 2026. They noted that the account page layouts needed to remain standardized to provide a cohesive user experience.
* UI Cleanup and Redundancy: Parth Goda Rajesh identified significant text redundancy on the dashboards, such as repetitive labeling for stages like "outreach" and "out-of-cycle." The team agreed to remove this clutter to improve readability and save space, as the repetitive text wasted screen real estate and made navigation less efficient.
* Code Repository Conflict and Restoration: A technical conflict arose where the standardized account page layout was inadvertently overwritten due to overlapping code pushes. Dean Liang, Parth Goda Rajesh, and Aaron Rose worked together to identify the correct commit, revert the changes, and restore the centralized account page view. The team reaffirmed the policy of halting code pushes until after the upcoming demo to prevent further instability.
